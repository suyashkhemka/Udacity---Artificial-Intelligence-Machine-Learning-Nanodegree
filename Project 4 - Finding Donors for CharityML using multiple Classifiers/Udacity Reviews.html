comment: Here I would like to emphasize the importance of beta value in the f-beta score metric. Remember that depending on the beta value the fbeta score will prioritize precision or recall over the other. The selection of beta will depend on your final goal. Below you can find an illustration of the idea:

  • Recall focus on avoiding false negatives FN. Namely, the less FN the higher the recall score.
  • Precision focus on avoiding False positives FP. Namely, the less FP the higher the precision score.

missing requirement: It is a requirement to set up random_state since this parameter will let us reproduce the results you got in your analysis. If you don't specify the random_state in your code, then every time you run(execute) your code a new random value is generated and hence your models will output different results. However, if you assigned a value to it, something like random_state = 42 then no matter how many times you execute your code the result would be the same. I know this is kind of tricky but it helps to reproduce results, that's why it is called pseudo-random since under the hood you are setting a seed for the random generation. If you are interested in the topic you can find some information here
